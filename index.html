7. Risk assessment

Instructor concentration risk
Deniz (27% of revenue) and Selin (23%) are non-partner employees with no contractual retention. Either leaving takes ~25% of revenue immediately. Students follow instructors in this market. There are no non-compete clauses visible.
Competitive saturation in Batıkent
30+ studios in the catchment area. A new competitor can open with 4-6 reformers (~100-180K TL) and a rented space. No franchise, IP, or technology moat protects this business.
Rent escalation in high-inflation environment
70K TL/month rent. With Turkey's inflation, landlords may demand 30-50% annual increases. At 100K/month, margin drops ~13 percentage points. Lease terms and renewal conditions are unknown.
Many private (1-person) sessions = low revenue density
A significant portion of classes (especially Eylem and Begüm) serve only 1 student. These occupy a full time slot but generate only 863 TL vs 2,175 TL for a 6-person class. Revenue optimisation is possible but may upset existing private clients.
Weekend underutilisation (upside)
Saturday & Sunday each run only 8 classes. S2 on weekends has just 2 classes. Adding 4-6 weekend classes could generate 30-50K TL/month incremental revenue with zero additional fixed cost.
